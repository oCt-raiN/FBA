Stoichiometric Matrix

The first step in FBA is to mathematically represent metabolic reactions. The core feature of this representation is a tabulation, in the form of a numerical matrix. Every row of this matrix represents a metabolite involved and every column represents a reaction. The entries in each column are the stoichiometric coefficients of the metabolites participating in a reaction. There is a negative coefficient for every metabolite consumed and a positive coefficient for every metabolite that is produced. A stoichiometric coefficient of zero is used for every metabolite that does not participate in a particular reaction.

Reference: Orth, J.D., Thiele, I. and Palsson, B.Ø. (2010) “What is Flux Balance Analysis?,” Nature Biotechnology, 28(3), pp. 245–248. Available at: https://doi.org/10.1038/nbt.1614.
